|---|---|---|---|---|

### Moat Verdict
Strong patent and scientific moat remain but are narrower than peak. Clinical inferiority of semaglutide vs tirzepatide (head-to-head trial, 2024) is the key moat breach. However:
- Novo has oral GLP-1 (amycretin oral in Phase 3) — potential to recapture market if efficacy superior
- Rare disease franchise provides stable, defensive revenue floor
- Novo Nordisk Foundation ownership prevents distress selling and provides patient capital for R&D recovery
- Geographic diversification (EM/Asia/EU) partially offsets US market share loss

### Investment Thesis (suggested summary)
Novo Nordisk is a world-class pharmaceutical business experiencing a classic drawdown from peak euphoria. The 56% decline from ATH reflects:
(1) Real competitive threat from Eli Lilly's superior tirzepatide in the US obesity market
(2) IRA Medicare pricing cuts in 2027 hitting the two biggest products
(3) CagriSema next-gen pipeline failure removing a key re-rating catalyst
(4) First-ever guidance for negative revenue growth

At ~12x forward earnings and ~8x EV/EBITDA — well below historical 25x P/E and peer median 15x P/E — the stock embeds meaningful pessimism. Bull case rests on oral amycretin success (Phase 3 2026-2027), EM market share gains, and the secular obesity/diabetes tailwind that remains structurally intact. Risk is further US pricing erosion and if oral pipeline also disappoints.

**RATING: HOLD** — Valuation is not demanding but near-term earnings headwinds are real. Monitor amycretin Phase 3 data as the primary re-rating catalyst.
